Spoken Jazz & the tingg Machine

A creative community ignites in a drab Navy town outside Seattle, adding color to the landscape & creating a renaissance despite the blue collar world.

Discover the story

The Story

In Bremerton, a Navy town painted in shades of grey, the paths of guitarist Bub Pratt and bassist Andrew converge at Olympic College. Bub, a scholarship student living a transient life fueled by poetry and improvisation, contrasts with the meticulous, focused Andrew, newly arrived and seeking structure. Despite their differences, their shared passion for jazz forges an unlikely bond, positioning them as anchors for a burgeoning creative scene.

"If he plays more, you play less." - Miles Davis

Amidst jam sessions fueled by Pizneon Greens and bootleg coffee, open mics at the divey Old Town Tavern, and the constant rhythm of Bub's vintage Remington typewriter – the *tingg* Machine – a community sparks.

They navigate disastrous gigs, complicated relationships (divorces, unexpected pregnancies, the magnetic pull of new connections like Mary and Karen), black market dealings orchestrated by the enigmatic Bill Lawson, and the raw, messy process of finding their artistic voices against a blue-collar backdrop.

It's a story about jazz, poetry, resilience, and the accidental renaissance ignited by cheap beer, ambition, and the refusal to let the landscape remain colorless.

The World: Rat City Rhapsody

The air hangs thick with the smell of brine and rain. This isn't Seattle's polish; it's Bremerton, Silverdale, Kingston – working towns huddled by the Puget Sound. Life revolves around the Navy base, the ferry schedules, and the local dives.

Old Town Tavern

Home of Pizneon Greens and Fred's crooner past. A divey watering hole with sticky floors, dim lights, and unpredictable open mic nights that somehow became the heartbeat of an evolving scene.

Olympic College

A haven where the music department's practice rooms echo with scales and standards, a stark contrast to the "drab Navy town" outside. Site of scholarships, mentorships, and technical mastery.

Scott Street House

Ground zero for late-night jams and chaotic camaraderie. A shared home becoming an accidental creative hub, with walls absorbing melodies and arguments alike.

The *tingg* Machine

Central to Bub's world is his late 40's Remington portable typewriter, the tingg* Machine. It's not just a tool for his zines; it's a percussion instrument, a character, the relentless rhythm section for his spoken jazz dreams.

Its *tingg* punctuates the narrative, a mechanical heartbeat in the digital age. Worn keys, sticky ribbon, the satisfying clack of thoughts becoming tangible – the machine embodies Bub's approach to art: imperfect, analog, refusing to be silenced.

Bill's Grotto

Part speakeasy, part black market hub. A hidden venue run by the enigmatic Bill Lawson, where contraband and creativity intersect in dimly-lit corners.

The Sound: Improvisation & Ink

The soundtrack is a raw blend of Bebop energy, Cool Jazz introspection, and the rhythmic cadence of spoken word poetry hammered out on a typewriter. Think Miles Davis meets Kerouac in a rainy port town.

Jazz Influences

The music leans heavily on improvisation, complex harmonies, and the driving rhythms characteristic of Bebop, but with moments of lyrical Cool Jazz reflection.

  • Charlie Parker
  • Dizzy Gillespie
  • Miles Davis (early works)
  • Thelonious Monk
  • Chet Baker

Spoken Word & Poetry

Bub's poetry, often performed alongside the music, adds a layer of narrative and percussive texture. The *tingg* of his typewriter is an integral part of the soundscape.

  • Beat Generation poets
  • Free verse rhythm
  • Observational & confessional
  • The *tingg* Machine

Overall Vibe

Expect energetic jams, smoky ballads, frantic poetic bursts, and the ever-present sound of rain and the nearby shipyard – a uniquely Bremerton blend.

  • Late night sessions
  • Dive bar acoustics
  • Creative tension
  • Analog warmth
